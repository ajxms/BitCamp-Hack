🔊 ECHO
Your voice is your weapon. Your frequency is your key.

What is Echo?
Deep underground, a shadowy organization has been stealing voices — silencing people, one by one. But voices aren't just sound. In the right hands, they're a force of nature.
You are one of the few who still has theirs.
Echo is a 2D puzzle platformer where sound is your only tool. Fire waves at walls, tune your frequency with a physical dial, and use the laws of acoustics to outsmart enemies, shatter barriers, and navigate a labyrinthine base — one echo at a time.

🎮 Gameplay
The entire game revolves around one core question: what frequency do you need?
Two wave types. Completely different behaviors. Every puzzle forces you to choose.
〰️ Low Frequency
High penetration · Low reflection
Rumbles through solid walls. Passes through stone and heavy obstacles like they aren't there. Use it to trigger hidden switches, interact with mechanisms behind cover, and feel out the environment without breaking it.
⚡ High Frequency
Low penetration · High reflection  ·  θᵢ = θᵣ
Bounces off hard surfaces with perfect precision. Shatters glass. Ricochets around corners. Use it to reach targets in acoustic shadows — places your eyes can see but your waves can't reach directly.
The catch? Every room is a puzzle. Every wall is a decision.

🕹️ Hardware Controller
This is where it gets interesting.
Instead of pressing a button to switch frequencies, you turn a dial.
A physical potentiometer wired to an Arduino lets you smoothly tune between low and high frequency in real time — the same way you'd tune an old radio. It feels analog. It feels tactile. It feels like you're actually manipulating sound.
